Upstanding by Design:

Encouraging Bystanders of Cyberbullying

  •  2017    
  •  Cornell Social Media Lab

Project Info

This experiment controls user interface elements on EatSnap.Love social platform to establish connection between the bystanders and the victim and place responsibility on bystanders. Participants will be randomly put into one of the six conditions with different amount of notifications of people reading their posts and whether the user interface reflects people are notified when participants have read their posts.

Researcher:

  •  Dominic DiFranzo
EatSnap.Love

Education:

Responsive Environment for Media Literacy

  •  2017    
  •  Cornell Social Media Lab

Project Info

Media Literacy is the ability to access, analyze, evaluate and create media in a variety of forms. Today technology has made it easy for people to access information and create media, and children are exposed to a huge amount of information from a variety of sources. In this project, an online platform learnlove.life is used to teach K-12 students about media literacy and how to use soial media safely and responsively.

More specifically, it aims to teach students about:

  • Safe posting and self-presentation
  • Cyberbullying
  • Digital literacy information

Researcher:

  •  Jessie Taft
Learnlove.Life
